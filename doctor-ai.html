Important: AI tools in healthcare are decision support systems — not replacements for clinical judgment, diagnosis, or treatment decisions. All AI outputs must be reviewed and validated by a qualified clinician. Regulatory compliance (MHRA, FDA, CE marking) applies to clinical AI tools.

😨 MYTHS vs REALITY
😰AI will replace doctorsFEAR
Medicine requires touch, intuition, contextual judgment, and human connection. AI excels at pattern recognition in data — not at sitting with a frightened patient or navigating clinical complexity. Doctors who use AI will outperform those who don't.
😤AI diagnoses are unreliableFEAR
FDA-approved AI tools like IDx-DR for diabetic retinopathy and AI ECG interpretation have demonstrated accuracy matching or exceeding specialists in specific tasks. The key is narrow, validated, well-regulated applications — not general AI.
✅AI saves clinical time for patientsFACT
Doctors spend up to 50% of their time on documentation. AI ambient scribes (like Nuance DAX) listen to the consultation and write the clinical note automatically — giving that time back to patients.

🗺️ YOUR AI LEARNING ROADMAP
1
Understand What AI Actually Is (Week 1)

Read the INTRO and LLMs tabs on this site. Understanding what AI can and cannot do is the foundation for safe, effective clinical use. Takes 30 minutes.

2
Try an AI Scribe in Practice (Week 2–3)

Many trusts and practices now offer Nuance DAX or similar tools. Even trying it in one clinic session reveals the time savings immediately. Your EHR team can help.

3
Use AI for Literature Review (Month 1)

Next time you have a clinical question, ask ChatGPT or Perplexity alongside PubMed. Compare the speed and quality. Always verify sources — AI can hallucinate citations.

4
Understand RAG for Clinical Knowledge (Month 2)

RAG (Retrieval Augmented Generation) is how hospitals build AI systems over their own guidelines and protocols. See the RAG tab — understanding this helps you evaluate vendor AI tools critically.

5
Lead AI Governance in Your Department (Month 3+)

Clinicians must be at the table when AI tools are evaluated and deployed. Your patient safety perspective is irreplaceable in procurement, ethics review, and staff training.
